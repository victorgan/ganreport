Part 1: The Math That Governs Everything

The Damage Formula

Your total damage is the product of five multiplicative layers. Because they multiply, improving the weakest layer gives the biggest returns.

Layer 1 — Level Multiplier: Each level multiplies base damage by 1.1. Level 5 = 1.46× level 1 — a 46% boost before items. Reaching level 5 (and your Ultimate) before the chapter boss is the single most important thing in the game.

Layer 2 — DMG Stat: Each point = +1% to all ability damage. 50 DMG = +50%. 100 DMG = double.

Layer 3 — Status: Strength = 1.25× dealt. Weaken = 0.75× dealt. Binary on/off, multiplicative with everything else.

Layer 4 — Crits: Base 5% chance. Multiplier = 1.5 + Crit Damage bonus. Crit chance above 100% overflows into extra crit damage (+0.01 per 1% overflow).

This is not a smooth curve — four discrete tiers. Most players undervalue this layer.

Key Insight
Staggering an enemy from Light Armor to Stunned doubles your effective damage. Heavy → Stunned quadruples it. Boss stagger windows are 2–4× damage multipliers — everything between staggers operates at a fraction of your real output.

The Health Formula

Max HP = Base HP × 1.1^(Level-1) × (1 + Vitality/100) × HP Multipliers

Vitality does double duty: each 10 Vitality = ~10% more HP AND +1 DMG with Ogre Blood. Healing fountains give +10 Vitality each — simultaneously offensive and defensive.

Armor & Cooldown Reduction

Armor: Exact formula undocumented (wiki: "TBA"). Diminishing returns. Useful up to ~40 as a one-shot safety net. Beyond that, invest in DMG or Vitality. Exception: Dragon Hide 4× converts Armor → 40% DMG.

CDR Stacking: Multiplicative, not additive. Diminishing absolute returns per copy.

Part 3: Talent Strategy

One talent per level-up. Rarities: Common → Rare → Epic → Legendary. Upgrade rarity at Sandman for Shards. The Epic→Legendary jump is significant. Sun Crown (Merlin DLC) auto-upgrades all talents.

Before the Run

Know your hero's 2–3 strongest talents. Know which ability to build around (Power/Special/Attack). Know which items you want.

During the Run

  1. Take the strongest talent offered, not the one that fits your plan. Adaptation wins.
  2. Use Stars of Fate to reroll bad offerings — most valuable early.
  3. Upgrade core talents to Legendary at Sandman before buying new items.

Talent Priority at Each Level-Up

  1. Quest talents with DMG or Crit (always take if strong)
  2. Talents synergizing with your current best ability
  3. New mechanics (AoE, lifesteal, status application)
  4. Generic stat boosts (only if nothing better)

For pivot/adaptation guidance, see Part 10: Mid-Run Decisions.

Part 4: The Nightmare Economy — Time vs. Power

The 18-Minute Clock

Each chapter gives 18 minutes before the boss awakens. The Hourglass of Dreams system lets you trigger bosses early for bonus rewards. Community data: ~77% fail rate with <6 min prep, ~83% success with full timer.

Optimal Route

Phase 1: Vision
18:00 → 14:00
Crow Towers + Green POIs
Phase 2: Power
14:00 → 8:00
Yellow POIs, Fountains, Wells, Grimoires, Dungeon
Phase 3: Quest
8:00 → 0:00
Chapter Quest, Red POIs, Tumors (Ch3)

Sandman Spending Priority

  1. Upgrade best talent to Legendary
  2. Buy core scaling item (Ogre Blood, Golden Egg)
  3. Upgrade second talent
  4. Supporting items

Death & Revival

At 0 HP you enter Death's Door — downed but revivable. Solo: auto-revive with a feather (limited, fewer on higher difficulty). Co-op: teammates manually revive or spend a feather. Aladdin's Green Wish revives the entire team. The Altar of Heroes costs 100 Shards per player.

Chapter 3: The Claws (Hardest Fight)

Community Consensus
Harder than the final boss. Do both Nightmare Tumors first (−40% HP on both entities).

Linked Stagger: Both have 50% DR. One staggers → gets 100% DR until other also staggers. Both must stagger simultaneously for vulnerability.

Linked Death: One at 0 HP → other stunned. Don't kill second fast enough → first revives at ~25%.

Combined Spinning Laser: Both move to edge → run to center → spiral outward following rotation.

Epilogue: Baba Yaga

Phase 1: Kill skulls → boss vulnerable → DPS → repeat.

Phase 2: Rotating laser, red markers, full arena nova — requires Defense/Intangible (dash alone insufficient). Witch's Broom also works.

If scaled properly, often easier than Claws (single target). Save Defense for nova.

Part 8: Item Build Templates

Ten archetypes. Adapt to what the game offers — these are decision trees, not prescriptions.

Build 1: The Vitality Stacker (Universal, Highest Win Rate)
Ogre Blood → Philosopher's Stone → Chainmail + Mermaid Tears + Horn of Plenty

Luxury: Bloody Mary's Mirror (build becomes broken)

Math: 100 Vit = +10 DMG from Ogre. With Mirror, 100 Vit = +60 DMG total (but HP halved).

Works with: Every hero. Commit: Found Ogre Blood in Chapter 1.

Build 2: The Glass Cannon (Ability Focus)
Excalibur → Raven Effigy + Raven CDR + Card + Stone

Power example: King of Clubs + Raven Claw + Flaming Pearl + Excalibur + Effigy.

Works with: Scarlet, Beowulf, Snow Queen, Wukong. Commit: Effigy or Excalibur early.

Build 3: The Armor Converter (Dragon Hide)
Dragon Hide ×4 → Green Armor → Ogre Blood

Math: 60 Armor × 40% = +24 free DMG. Never force this. Fall into it.

Works with: Beowulf. Commit: Found 2+ Dragon Hides in Chapter 1.

Build 4: The Economy Snowball (Aladdin-Specific)
Mad Hat → Horn of Plenty → Ogre Blood → ∞

Loop: Damage → Shards → Wish → Items → Heal → Repeat. Scales infinitely.

Works with: Aladdin only. Commit: Mad Hat + Master Thief.

Build 5: The CDR Machine (Ability Spam)
Raven Claw + Raven Beak + Raven Eye → Seven-League Boots → Raven Effigy

CDR math: 2× Raven = 43.75%. 3× = 57.8%. Raven Eye 3× = Ult clears all other CDs.

Works with: Wukong, Scarlet, Snow Queen. Commit: 2+ Raven pieces + Boots.

Build 6: The Crit Stacker
Ogre Blood ×5 → Eternal Rose ×5 → Card ×4

Luxury: Devil's Pocket (+50% Crit after 400 shards)

Math at full: 5% + 20% Ogre + 10% Rose + 40% Card + 50% Devil's = 125% crit → always crit with +25% overflow. Multiplier: 2.25× every hit.

Works with: Scarlet (Critical Hunter), Beowulf (Blazing Runes). Commit: Ogre + Eternal Rose in Ch1.

Build 7: The Cursed Build (High Risk)
Oni Mask + Nightmare Thorn + Witch's Broom → Chainmail

Math: 3 Cursed = +45 DMG from Oni + doubled Power + Intangible dashes. Chainmail is mandatory.

Avoid: Legendary items (Oni penalizes −10 Armor each).

Works with: Aladdin, Wukong. Commit: Oni Mask from first dungeon + Chainmail from quest.

Build 8: The Sustain Tank (Co-op Support)
Holy Grail → Mermaid Tears → Chainmail → Ogre Blood

Math: 3× Mermaid Tears → Holy Grail heal becomes 17.5 HP. Raven Skull reduces CD 25% = ~33% more healing.

Works with: Melusine, Beowulf. Commit: Co-op + Holy Grail from quest + Mermaid Tears.

Build 9: The Boss Killer (Single Target)
Voodoo Doll ×5 → Ogre Blood → Excalibur + Ring of Dispel

Math: Ring of Dispel Vulnerable vs Light Armor: 50% DR → ~25% = 50% real damage increase.

Works with: Wukong, Scarlet, Juliet. Commit: Voodoo Doll early + single-target hero.

Build 10: The Speedrunner (Map Efficiency)
Fast-Walker Boots → Raven Wing ×3 → Seven-League Boots → Ogre Blood

Math: 2 extra fountains/chapter × 3 chapters = +60 Vit = +6 DMG + 60% more HP.

Works with: Weak early heroes (Wukong, Geppetto). Commit: Fast-Walker + Raven Wing early.

Part 10: Mid-Run Decision Framework

When to Stay

  • 2+ core items by mid-Chapter 1
  • Talents support intended ability
  • Hitting benchmarks: 15+ DMG by Ch1 boss, 40+ by Ch2

When to Pivot

  • End of Ch1 with 0 copies of intended core items
  • Strong talents for a different ability than planned
  • Found build-defining Legendary/Cursed that doesn't match plan

How to Pivot

  1. Identify what the game IS giving you
  2. Find closest build template from Part 8
  3. Stop investing in old plan — don't waste Stars rerolling
  4. A coherent B-tier build beats a half-assembled S-tier build. Always.
Universal Fallback
If nothing comes together: Vitality Stacker. Ogre Blood is Common, every fountain feeds it, works with every hero.

Part 11: Meta-Skills & Resources

Spacing Discipline

The single most important skill. Treat boss fights like a bullet hell. Optimizing builds is secondary to not getting hit.

The Real Difficulty Curve

Chapter 1 is where most Nightmare runs die. If you survive Act 1 with decent items, Acts 2–3 get easier as scaling snowballs. The Claws in Ch3 is the exception — hard regardless of power until you learn patterns.
