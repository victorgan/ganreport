1 Minute 60 seconds
The length of a decision. Enough to change a life, not enough to overthink it.
Peak Human
Inge Lehmann identified an inner core of the Earth — the key insight struck while reviewing a single seismogram printout.
Breakthroughs often arrive in minutes after years of preparation.
Grandmaster Magnus Carlsen can evaluate ~3–5 chess positions deeply, each with 10+ move lookahead.
In bullet chess (1 min total), he still plays at ~2700+ Elo, stronger than 99.99% of humans with unlimited time.
An elite pianist sight-reads ~4 pages of complex sheet music in 60 seconds.
Requires parallel processing: reading ahead, fingering, dynamics, pedaling — all simultaneously.
An ER trauma surgeon triages and initiates life-saving intervention within 60 seconds of patient arrival.
The "golden minute" in trauma medicine — every second of delay increases mortality.
Usain Bolt covers 600+ meters at full sprint pace. His 100m takes just 9.58s; in a minute he could run ~5.5 laps of a 100m straight.
A world-class speed cuber solves a Rubik's cube ~10 times. Record single solve: 3.13 seconds.
Realistic Human
Read ~250 words — about one page of a book.
Average adult reading speed is ~238 wpm. A minute of reading is roughly 1 Kindle "page."
Type ~40–70 words — a solid paragraph or a short email.
Average typing speed is ~40 wpm; a good typist hits 70+.
Brush your teeth (though dentists want you to do 2 minutes).
Walk ~80 meters — about the length of a city block.
Average walking speed: ~1.4 m/s = 84m per minute.
Have one complete thought and say it out loud — about 2–3 spoken sentences.
Decide what to eat from a restaurant menu (on a good day).
5 Minutes 300 seconds
The smallest block of real work. Long enough to make progress, short enough to not need a plan.
Peak Human
Srinivasa Ramanujan would fill half a page with novel mathematical identities in minutes — results that took other mathematicians weeks to verify.
A concert pianist performs an entire Chopin Ballade movement from memory with full emotional arc.
An elite simultaneous interpreter at the UN translates ~750 words across languages in real-time, maintaining nuance and diplomatic tone.
A world-class auctioneer sells 25–30 lots at Sotheby's, managing bids from a global audience, each sale in ~10 seconds.
Eliud Kipchoge runs 1.4 km — nearly a full mile — at marathon world record pace (2:01:09).
Realistic Human
Make your bed and tidy a nightstand.
Write and send a short email — subject line, 3 sentences, sign-off.
Brew coffee from bean to cup (pour-over or espresso).
Do a meaningful micro-workout: 50 push-ups, 50 squats, or 3 sets of pull-ups.
Research shows "exercise snacks" of 5 min have measurable cardiovascular benefit.
Review and merge a small PR — read the diff, check tests, approve.
Meditate. 5 minutes of focused breathing measurably reduces cortisol.
15 Minutes 900 seconds
The "Pomodoro half." Enough for a complete unit of focused work.
Peak Human
Martin Luther King Jr. delivered the "I Have a Dream" speech in ~17 minutes — arguably the most impactful quarter-hour of American oratory.
Steve Jobs' iPhone launch demo — the first 15 minutes where he unveiled a phone, an iPod, and an internet device — then revealed they were one device.
Rehearsed for weeks, but the cultural impact landed in minutes.
A top TED speaker delivers a talk that gets 50M+ views. TED's format is explicitly built around the 15–18 minute sweet spot.
Sir Ken Robinson's "Do Schools Kill Creativity?" is the most-viewed TED talk ever.
An elite surgeon performs a complete laparoscopic appendectomy. Average is 30–45 min; the fastest are under 15.
Terence Tao reportedly solved competition math problems in minutes that stumped PhD students for hours.
Realistic Human
Shower and get dressed.
Cook a simple meal — scrambled eggs, rice and beans, a stir-fry with prepped ingredients.
Read a long-form article (~3,000 words, like a good Atlantic or New Yorker piece).
Write 200–400 words of first-draft prose if you know what you want to say.
Walk a mile at a comfortable pace.
Complete a focused code task — fix a clear bug, write a utility function, refactor a method.
Learn 10–15 flashcards in Anki with full review.
30 Minutes 1,800 seconds
One Pomodoro. The atomic unit of deep work.
Peak Human
Lin-Manuel Miranda wrote the opening number of Hamilton in about 30 minutes on a train, after the core concept clicked.
The melody and structure of "Alexander Hamilton" came in a single sitting.
John Lennon wrote "Across the Universe" in a single sitting of about 30 minutes, lyrics flowing without revision.
A world-class 10K runner finishes the entire race. Joshua Cheptegei's world record: 26:11.
An elite lawyer drafts a complete contract clause that took the junior associate 4 hours.
Pattern recognition from thousands of prior contracts compresses the work.
A senior staff engineer reviews a complex system design doc and identifies the 3 critical flaws.
Realistic Human
A solid gym session — 5 working sets of a compound lift plus accessories.
30 min of focused lifting is more effective than 90 min of phone-checking.
Cook a proper dinner — pasta from scratch sauce, a curry, a sheet pan meal.
Write ~500–800 words of decent first-draft content.
A meaningful conversation — the kind where you actually connect, not small talk.
Run a 5K — average recreational runner finishes in 28–35 min.
Complete a standup + one focused task in a sprint.
1 Hour 3,600 seconds
The natural unit of a meeting, a workout, a commute. The block everyone claims to not have enough of.
Peak Human
Mozart reportedly composed the overture to Don Giovanni in a single hour the night before the premiere, while chatting with friends.
The anecdote is debated, but contemporary accounts confirm the speed was extraordinary.
Picasso could complete a full painting in an hour. When asked why one sketch cost $5,000: "It took me 40 years and one hour."
Eliud Kipchoge runs a half-marathon (21.1 km / 13.1 mi). His WR pace covers that distance in ~60 min.
A world-class coder in competitive programming solves 3–4 hard algorithmic problems (Codeforces Div 1 level).
Paul Graham wrote that his best essays took about an hour of actual writing — after days of thinking.
The leverage is in the preparation; the hour is just transcription of pre-formed thought.
A master sushi chef prepares ~50–60 pieces of omakase — each one a lifetime of technique compressed into seconds.
Realistic Human
A full gym workout — warm-up, 4–5 exercises, cool-down.
Write 1,000–1,500 words of a blog post or report draft.
Deep-clean one room — kitchen or bathroom, thoroughly.
Watch a meaningful lecture and take notes (1 MIT OpenCourseWare session).
Complete a real coding task — implement a feature, write tests, submit a PR.
Commute to work — US average is 27.6 minutes each way.
Grocery shop — drive, shop, drive home, put away.
3 Hours 10,800 seconds
A morning. A focused session. The length of a movie, or the time between meals.
Peak Human
Isaac Asimov could produce a complete short story (~5,000 words, polished) in a single 3-hour morning session.
He published 500+ books in his career, writing daily 3-hour blocks.
Ernest Hemingway's daily output was typically one 3-hour morning session — producing 500–1,000 words of final prose.
"The best way is always to stop when you are going good."
A chess grandmaster plays and wins a full classical game — 40+ moves with deep calculation at each.
A neurosurgeon performs a complete tumor resection (simple cases). Complex ones take 6–12 hours.
An elite marathon runner finishes a full 26.2-mile marathon. Kelvin Kiptum's WR: 2:00:35.
Realistic Human
Watch a long movie — Lord of the Rings extended, Oppenheimer, Killers of the Flower Moon.
Deep work: write 2,000–3,000 words of a first draft, or produce a complete short blog post.
Complete a substantial coding task — design, implement, test a small feature end-to-end.
Bake bread from scratch — mix, knead, proof, shape, bake (with some waiting).
Assemble an IKEA bookshelf from box to books-on-shelf.
Complete a full Zwift cycling session — 60–90 min of structured intervals plus warm-up/cool-down.
8 Hours 28,800 seconds
One work day. One sleep. The rhythm of industrial civilization.
Peak Human
Jack Kerouac typed the entire scroll draft of "On the Road" in 3 weeks — roughly 6,000 words per 8-hour day on a continuous roll of paper.
A top competitive programmer solves 8–12 problems in an ICPC-style contest, including problems rated 2400+ on Codeforces.
Yannick Sener (speedrun) world records in full-length games are often 4–8 hour endurance tests of perfect execution.
A world-class surgeon performs 4–6 complex surgeries in a day.
An elite concert pianist practices 6–8 hours: scales, études, repertoire, and performance run-throughs.
Lang Lang, Yuja Wang, and others report 6–8 hour daily practice from childhood.
Realistic Human
~4 hours of actual deep work — Cal Newport's research suggests most knowledge workers max out at 4 hours of cognitively demanding work per day.
The rest is meetings, email, context-switching, and recovery.
Read 50,000–60,000 words — roughly an entire short novel (The Great Gatsby is ~47,000 words).
Drive ~500 miles — Seattle to San Francisco, or NYC to Cincinnati.
Complete a full sprint's worth of story points — 5–8 points for an average dev day (if uninterrupted, which never happens).
Move apartments (small apartment, with help, no major furniture).
Sleep through a full cycle — 4–5 REM cycles, memory consolidation, growth hormone release.
1 Day 86,400 seconds
One full rotation of Earth. The container for everything you are.
Peak Human
Dean Karnazes ran 350 miles (560 km) in 80 hours 44 minutes — that's ~105 miles (170 km) per 24-hour period.
In a single 24-hour hackathon, small teams have built complete working products — GroupMe was built at a TechCrunch hackathon in ~24 hours.
Stephen King writes ~2,000 polished words per day, every single day, including holidays. Over decades, this produced 65+ novels.
An ER doctor on a 24-hour shift treats 30–50 patients, making potentially hundreds of diagnostic decisions.
A day trader at a top firm executes thousands of trades managing tens of millions in capital.
Realistic Human
Sleep 7–8 hours, work 8, commute 1, eat/hygiene 2 — leaving ~3–5 hours of actual free time.
This is the real budget. Most self-improvement plans fail because they ignore it.
Hike a significant trail — 15–20 miles, a full day in the mountains.
Deep-clean an entire apartment.
Fly across the country — door to door including transit, security, flight, arrival.
Read 2–3 chapters of a textbook with notes, or an entire popular nonfiction book.
1 Week 604,800 seconds
A sprint. A vacation. Long enough to ship, short enough to remember every day.
Peak Human
Einstein wrote his paper on special relativity in roughly 5–6 weeks, but the core mathematical framework came together in about a week of furious writing in June 1905.
The Wright Brothers went from first powered flight (Dec 14, failed) to the historic 59-second flight in 3 days of iteration.
Faulkner wrote "As I Lay Dying" in six weeks, claiming the first draft took just 6 days of night shifts at a power plant.
A YC startup team ships 3–5 iterations of their MVP in a single week during batch.
Apollo 13 engineers designed and tested the CO₂ scrubber fix in ~2 days using only materials available on the spacecraft.
Realistic Human
Complete one sprint — 3–5 tasks, a demo, a retro.
Read an entire book — 30–60 min per day = a 300-page book by Sunday.
Establish a micro-routine — 7 consecutive days of a habit starts building neural pathways.
Plan and take a short vacation — fly somewhere, explore for 4 days, return.
Learn the basics of a new tool — VS Code shortcuts, a new CLI tool, basic Vim motions.
Recover from a hard training block — muscle repair, glycogen replenishment, neural adaptation.
1 Month ~2.6M seconds
The billing cycle. The habit horizon. One lunar orbit.
Peak Human
NaNoWriMo winners write 50,000 words (a full novel draft) in 30 days. Some published authors do this routinely.
That's ~1,667 words/day. Hemingway would have scoffed at the quality, but the output is real.
Notch built the first playable version of Minecraft in about a month (May 2009), working in Java.
The Manhattan Project assembled the Trinity device in roughly 4 weeks at the final stage.
Picasso's Guernica — from first sketch to completed 11×25 foot masterpiece in ~5 weeks.
A top bodybuilder on a prep cut loses 8–12 lbs of fat in a month while preserving muscle mass.
Realistic Human
Form a habit — research suggests 21–30 days for simple habits (drinking water), longer for complex ones.
Ship a medium feature — design, implement, test, code review, deploy, monitor.
Lose 4–8 lbs of fat at a healthy 1–2 lbs/week rate.
Read 3–4 books at a book-per-week pace.
Complete an online course — Coursera, Udemy, or a MOOC at ~5 hrs/week.
See first visible fitness gains — muscle tone, improved run times, lower resting heart rate.
Fingernails grow ~3mm. You are literally a different physical object.
3 Months ~7.9M seconds
A quarter. A season. The minimum viable transformation.
Peak Human
The first iPhone prototype went from "let's put a phone in the iPod" to a working demo in roughly one quarter of focused work by a small team.
A YC batch (3 months) has produced companies worth billions — Stripe, Airbnb, and Dropbox all went through 3-month batches.
Dostoevsky dictated "The Gambler" in 26 days to meet a contract deadline — and it's considered one of his finest novellas. He wrote "The Idiot" across a quarter.
A professional athlete's off-season training block — this is how you add 15 lbs to a bench press or shave 10 seconds off a mile time.
SpaceX's Starship prototype cycles from design changes to test flight are roughly quarterly.
Realistic Human
Dramatically change your physique — 12-week programs exist because this is the minimum for visible, significant body recomposition.
Go from zero to half-marathon with a structured training plan (Couch to Half).
Learn a new programming language well enough to be productive — not expert, but shipping code.
Complete a bootcamp — most coding bootcamps are 12–16 weeks.
Read 10–12 books. Bill Gates reads ~50/year; 12 in a quarter is ambitious but doable.
Build and ship a side project — a personal website, a small app, a Chrome extension.
6 Months ~15.8M seconds
Half a year. Long enough to forget what it felt like before.
Peak Human
Einstein's "Annus Mirabilis" — in roughly 6 months (March–September 1905) he published 4 papers that redefined physics: photoelectric effect, Brownian motion, special relativity, and E=mc².
The original Macintosh team went from concept to working prototype in about 6 months under Steve Jobs.
An elite marathoner completes a full training block — base building, speed work, tapering — for a peak race performance.
A Series A startup goes from product-market fit signals to a fundable growth trajectory in ~6 months.
Realistic Human
Reach conversational level in a new language — with daily practice using immersion methods (Pimsleur, tutoring, apps).
Complete a college semester — 4–5 courses, finals, the whole arc.
Train for and run a marathon from a solid running base.
Change jobs — network, apply, interview, negotiate, give notice, start.
Pay off a small debt — $3,000–$5,000 with aggressive budgeting.
Become "pretty good" at a new skill — guitar chords, cooking techniques, a new sport. Not expert, but visibly competent.
1 Year ~31.5M seconds (π × 10⁷)
One orbit around the sun. People overestimate what they can do in a year, and underestimate what they can do in five.
Peak Human
Tolkien spent a year writing The Hobbit (on and off). Shakespeare wrote 2–3 plays per year during his peak period.
A founding team goes from idea to seed-funded startup with users. Instagram launched ~8 weeks after pivoting from Burbn, but the full arc was about a year.
OpenAI went from GPT-3 to ChatGPT in roughly a year (mid-2021 training → Nov 2022 launch), changing the trajectory of AI.
A PhD student publishes their first significant paper — typically year 2, but the best publish in year 1.
An Olympic athlete's training year — periodized blocks leading to a single peak performance lasting seconds or minutes.
Realistic Human
Read 20–50 books — at 30 min/day, you'll finish ~25 books/year.
That's more than most people read in a decade.
Lose 50+ lbs safely (~1 lb/week). Or gain 10–15 lbs of muscle (first year of lifting = newbie gains).
Build meaningful proficiency in a new domain — data science, product management, woodworking, music production.
Save $10,000–$20,000 with disciplined budgeting on a median US salary.
A baby goes from immobile blob to walking, babbling human — the most dramatic year of any life.
Complete 1,000 Anki reviews at 3/day — enough to meaningfully learn a language's core vocabulary.
3 Years ~94.5M seconds
The startup window. The PhD arc. Long enough to become genuinely good at something.
Peak Human
The Apollo Program went from Kennedy's speech (1961) to first lunar orbit (1964) in 3 years, with 400,000 people working in parallel.
Beethoven composed the 9th Symphony over roughly 3 years (1822–1824) — while completely deaf.
A world-class startup goes from founding to Series B. Stripe took ~3 years to reach $100M in payment volume.
Darwin spent 3 years on the HMS Beagle voyage (1831–1834) collecting the observations that would lead to natural selection.
Realistic Human
Complete a law degree (JD) or a significant portion of a PhD.
Go from beginner to intermediate in a musical instrument — playing songs, reading music, jamming with others.
Reach conversational fluency in a foreign language with consistent practice.
Get promoted one level at a large tech company (e.g. L4 → L5 at Google).
The median time to L5 is ~3 years at L4. Impact documentation and visibility matter as much as output.
Build a $50K–$100K investment portfolio from consistent monthly contributions + market returns.
A toddler becomes a kindergartener — from barely talking to reading simple books, riding a bike, having friendships.
5 Years ~158M seconds
The underestimated horizon. Most life-changing things happen in this window.
Peak Human
The Manhattan Project — from the Einstein-Szilard letter (1939) to Trinity (1945): 6 years to build a weapon that ended a world war and started an era.
SpaceX went from founding (2002) to first successful Falcon 1 orbit (2008) in 6 years, after 3 failures.
Amazon went from Jeff Bezos's garage (1994) to IPO (1997) to category dominance in books by ~1999.
Michelangelo painted the Sistine Chapel ceiling in 4 years (1508–1512), lying on his back on scaffolding.
A PhD student completes their dissertation — from coursework through quals, research, and defense.
Realistic Human
Complete a bachelor's degree (4–5 years) — the most common 5-year project in modern life.
Become genuinely skilled at a craft — 5 years of deliberate practice crosses the "people pay you for this" threshold.
Save for and buy a home — 5 years of saving for a down payment is a common timeline.
Build a meaningful professional network — 5 years in an industry creates durable connections.
Compound investments grow ~30–40% at historical market returns (7%/year). $50K becomes $70K.
A relationship goes from dating to deep partnership — shared experiences, crises navigated, trust built.
10 Years ~315M seconds
A decade. A career chapter. The "10,000 hour" horizon. You will not be the same person.
Peak Human
The 10,000-hour rule (popularized by Gladwell, researched by Ericsson): ~10 years of deliberate practice to reach world-class. The Beatles, Bill Gates, and chess grandmasters all put in roughly this duration.
Apple went from near-bankruptcy (1997) to the iPod-iPhone era (2007) in one decade under Jobs's return.
The Human Genome Project took 13 years (1990–2003) — the most ambitious biology project in history.
Tesla went from founding (2003) to the Model S (2012) — a decade from concept to industry disruption.
Tolkien spent ~12 years writing The Lord of the Rings (1937–1949).
Realistic Human
Two full career chapters — most people change roles or companies 2–3 times per decade.
A child goes from birth to 5th grade — from helpless infant to reading Harry Potter and having opinions.
Compound investments roughly double at 7%/year. $100K → $197K. Start early matters more than start big.
Your entire social circle turns over — research shows you replace about half your close friends every 7 years.
Your body replaces nearly all its cells — you are physically a different person than a decade ago.
Run 10,000+ miles if you average 20 miles/week. That's roughly Seattle to Tokyo and back.
25 Years ~788M seconds
A generation. The time between being parented and becoming a parent.
Peak Human
Warren Buffett built Berkshire Hathaway from a failing textile company (1965) to one of the world's most valuable conglomerates across ~25 years by 1990.
The internet went from ARPANET (1969) to the World Wide Web (1993) in ~24 years.
Darwin spent 20+ years refining the theory of evolution before publishing "On the Origin of Species" (1859). From Beagle observations to publication: ~25 years.
A Supreme Court justice shapes American law across 25+ year tenures.
Moore's Law over 25 years: ~16 doublings. A chip with 1M transistors in 1995 has 65B transistors by 2020.
Realistic Human
Raise a child from birth to adulthood. The single most common 25-year human project.
Pay off a mortgage — a 30-year mortgage is 83% done. A 15-year is long finished.
Build a full career — from entry-level to senior leadership, from junior dev to principal engineer or VP.
Compound investments grow ~5.4× at 7%/year. $100K → $542K. This is how normal people become millionaires.
Watch an entire city neighborhood transform — new buildings, new residents, different character.
A Lifetime ~50–80 productive years
Your total allocation. Non-renewable. The only resource that truly matters.
Peak Human
Leonardo da Vinci (67 years): painter, sculptor, architect, musician, mathematician, engineer, inventor, anatomist, geologist, botanist. Produced ~15 surviving paintings and 7,000+ pages of notebooks.
Euler (76 years): published ~866 papers and books — more than any mathematician in history. Averaged a paper per week in his most productive years. Continued producing after going blind.
Marie Curie (66 years): 2 Nobel Prizes in different sciences (Physics 1903, Chemistry 1911). Discovered radium and polonium, pioneered radiation therapy. Died from her own research.
Bach (65 years): composed 1,128 known works — cantatas, concertos, fugues, the Mass in B Minor. Most written while managing a church choir and raising 20 children (10 survived infancy).
Nikola Tesla (86 years): ~300 patents. Invented AC power systems, radio foundations, the Tesla coil, and envisioned wireless communication decades before WiFi.
Realistic Human
~80 years: 4,000 weeks. 29,000 days. You've already used a significant fraction.
~25 years sleeping, ~10 years working, ~7 years eating, ~4 years commuting, ~3 years on screens for entertainment.
That leaves ~31 years. Subtract childhood and old age, and you have maybe 20 years of peak productive freedom.
Build ~3 deep relationships — partner, 1–2 lifelong friends. Most people's inner circle is shockingly small.
Hold 8–12 jobs across 3–4 career phases.
Read ~2,000–3,000 books if you average one per week from age 20 to 75.
This sounds like a lot. It is not. There are ~130M books in existence.
Your heart beats ~3 billion times. Each one irretrievable.
100 Years ~3.15 × 10⁹ seconds
A century. Beyond any single life. The scale at which humanity itself is the protagonist.
What Humanity Accomplished
1903 → 2003: From the Wright Brothers' 12-second flight to the International Space Station orbiting permanently with a crew of 6.
1924 → 2024: Global life expectancy rose from ~34 years to ~73 years. We doubled the human lifespan in a century.
1925 → 2025: From no antibiotics (penicillin discovered 1928) to CRISPR gene editing, mRNA vaccines, and AI-designed proteins.
1945 → 2025: From ENIAC (one room, 5,000 additions/sec) to smartphones (pocket-sized, 15 trillion operations/sec). ~3 billion× improvement.
World GDP grew from ~$1T (1900) to ~$100T (2024). A hundred-fold increase in total economic output in one century.
What Persists
A well-built house lasts 100 years. A well-planted oak tree is just reaching maturity.
A family line extends ~4 generations. Your great-grandchildren may hear stories about you — but likely only your name.
A great book outlives its century. Shakespeare (400+ years), Homer (2,800+ years). But most books are forgotten within 20.
Languages shift noticeably. English speakers from 1925 would struggle with modern slang, abbreviations, and technical jargon.
Nearly every person alive today will be dead. The roster of humanity completely turns over. And the next century begins.
