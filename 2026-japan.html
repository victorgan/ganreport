Food Guide

Chain Restaurants (Kid-Friendly)

  • Kura Sushi — Conveyor belt sushi chain. Fun gachapon game for every 5 plates. Very kid-friendly.
  • Sushiro — Another popular conveyor belt sushi chain with good value and variety.
  • Ootoya — Home-style Japanese set meals (teishoku). Balanced, healthy options.
  • Yayoi Ken — Teishoku chain with set meals at reasonable prices.
  • Komeda Coffee — Popular coffee shop chain known for generous portions and morning sets (shiro-noir dessert).
  • Jonathan’s — Family restaurant chain with a wide menu including Japanese and Western dishes. Drink bar included.
  • Denny’s (Japan) — Different from North American Denny’s. Japanese-Western fusion family restaurant with kids menu.
  • Joyfull — Budget-friendly family restaurant chain with a large menu.

Recommended Dining Tips

  • Use Tabelog (the Japanese Yelp) to find highly-rated restaurants nearby
  • Look for teishoku (set meal) restaurants for balanced, affordable meals
  • Department store depachika (basement food halls) are great for takeaway bento, sweets, and fresh food — try Isetan, Seibu, or Roppongi Hills
  • Convenience stores (konbini) like 7-Eleven, Lawson, and FamilyMart have surprisingly good onigiri, sandwiches, and prepared meals — great for quick breakfasts
  • Ekiben (train bento) at major stations are a must for long rides — grab one at Tokyo Station before the Shinkansen
  • In Kawagoe, try unagi (freshwater eel) — a local specialty
  • In Uji, try matcha everything — the birthplace of Japanese green tea
  • In Osaka, try takoyaki (octopus balls), okonomiyaki (savory pancakes), and kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers) at Dotonbori

Shopping Guide

Clothing & Accessories

  • Kamakura Shirts — High-quality dress shirts at reasonable prices. Locations in Shinjuku, Ikebukuro, near Tokyo Station, and Osaka.
  • Il Regalo — Dress socks available at department stores (Isetan, Seibu). Beautiful patterns and quality.
  • Isetan Shinjuku Main Store — Premium department store. Men’s building has excellent sock and accessory selection.
  • Seibu Ikebukuro Main Store — Large department store in Ikebukuro with good sock and clothing departments.
  • Uniqlo — Tokyo Dome City location. Basics at great prices, Japan-exclusive items.
  • Bridlen — Dress shoe store, Tokyo only (Yurakucho Marui).

Electronics & Camera

  • Map Camera — Shinjuku’s premier used and new camera store. Great prices on lenses and bodies.
  • Bic Camera — Major electronics chain with locations in Shinjuku, Shibuya, Yurakucho, Nihombashi, Ueno, Akihabara, Ikebukuro.
  • Akihabara — Electronics district with countless specialty shops.

Kitchen & Home

  • Kappabashi Street — Kitchen Town. Japanese knives, ceramics, lacquerware, and plastic food samples.
  • Don Quijote — Discount store with everything from snacks to electronics to souvenirs. Open late.

Baby & Kids

  • Akachan Honpo — Major baby goods store. Diapers, clothing, toys, and feeding supplies.
  • Nishimatsuya — Budget baby and kids clothing and supplies.
  • Toys R Us — Still operating in Japan with good toy selection.
  • Matsumoto Kiyoshi — Drugstore chain with baby supplies and diapers at good prices.

Osaka Shopping

  • Shinsaibashi-suji — Long covered shopping arcade with fashion, cosmetics, and souvenirs.
  • Namba — Major shopping and entertainment district adjacent to Dotonbori.
